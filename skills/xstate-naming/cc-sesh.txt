⏺ Your instincts align well with XState conventions and the actor model philosophy. Here's how I think about it:                                                      
                                                                                                                                                                      
  States                                                                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Use "-ing" for async/pending states — this signals "in progress, waiting for something":                                                                            
  - loading, submitting, authenticating, fetching                                                                                                                     
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Use past participles or nouns for settled states:                                                                                                                   
  - loaded, authenticated, idle, error, success                                                                                                                       
  - Or descriptive nouns: ready, offline, unauthorized                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Actions                                                                                                                                                             
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Plain verbs work well — they describe what happens synchronously when a transition fires:                                                                           
  - assignUser, clearForm, logError, notifyParent                                                                                                                     
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Some prefix assign for context updates since that's the XState helper name, which creates consistency.                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Events                                                                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Past tense or imperative commands both have advocates:                                                                                                              
  - Past tense: USER_LOGGED_IN, FORM_SUBMITTED — describes what happened                                                                                              
  - Imperative: SUBMIT, LOGIN, RETRY — describes intent                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                      
  I lean toward imperative for user-initiated actions and past tense for external/system events. The key is consistency within a machine.                             
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Guards                                                                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                      
  This is where your boolean aversion gets tricky. Common patterns:                                                                                                   
  - hasPermission, isValid — the classic approach                                                                                                                     
  - canSubmit, shouldRetry — "can/should" reads more like capability                                                                                                  
  - Or just nouns: authorized, valid, empty                                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                      
  One caveat                                                                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                      
  State names appear in .matches() checks, so readability matters there too:                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                      
  // reads naturally                                                                                                                                                  
  if (state.matches('authenticating')) { ... }                                                                                                                        
  if (state.matches('authenticated')) { ... }                                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Your verb-based system sounds coherent. The main thing is internal consistency within each machine.

⏺ assign is XState's built-in action creator for updating context. The naming question is whether to reflect that in the action name itself.                          
                                                                                                                                                                      
  The assign function                                                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                      
  import { assign } from 'xstate';                                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                                      
  const machine = createMachine({                                                                                                                                     
    context: { user: null, attempts: 0 },                                                                                                                             
    // ...                                                                                                                                                            
    on: {                                                                                                                                                             
      LOGIN_SUCCESS: {                                                                                                                                                
        actions: assign({ user: (_, event) => event.user })                                                                                                           
      }                                                                                                                                                               
    }                                                                                                                                                                 
  });                                                                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Naming approaches                                                                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                                                      
  1. Prefix with assign — signals context mutation                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                                      
  const actions = {                                                                                                                                                   
    assignUser: assign({ user: (_, e) => e.user }),                                                                                                                   
    assignError: assign({ error: (_, e) => e.error }),                                                                                                                
    assignIncrementAttempts: assign({ attempts: (ctx) => ctx.attempts + 1 }),                                                                                         
  };                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Good: Scanning the machine, you immediately know what touches context.                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Bad: Gets awkward fast — assignFormClear, assignResetRetryCount. You're encoding implementation detail (that it uses assign) into the name.                         
                                                                                                                                                                      
  2. Verb-based — describes the intent                                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                      
  const actions = {                                                                                                                                                   
    setUser: assign({ user: (_, e) => e.user }),                                                                                                                      
    clearForm: assign({ form: null, errors: null }),                                                                                                                  
    incrementAttempts: assign({ attempts: (ctx) => ctx.attempts + 1 }),                                                                                               
    resetRetryCount: assign({ retryCount: 0 }),                                                                                                                       
  };                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Good: Reads naturally, focuses on what happens semantically.                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Bad: Can't tell at a glance if it's a context update or a side effect.                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                      
  3. Mixed — assign for simple setters, verbs for logic                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                      
  const actions = {                                                                                                                                                   
    // Simple property assignments                                                                                                                                    
    assignUser: assign({ user: (_, e) => e.user }),                                                                                                                   
    assignError: assign({ error: (_, e) => e.error }),                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                      
    // More complex operations get descriptive names                                                                                                                  
    clearForm: assign({ name: '', email: '', errors: null }),                                                                                                         
    mergeValidationErrors: assign({                                                                                                                                   
      errors: (ctx, e) => ({ ...ctx.errors, ...e.errors })                                                                                                            
    }),                                                                                                                                                               
  };                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
  This is a pragmatic middle ground but introduces inconsistency.                                                                                                     
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Side effects vs. context updates                                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                                      
  The distinction matters because XState has two kinds of actions:                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                                      
  const machine = createMachine({                                                                                                                                     
    // ...                                                                                                                                                            
    entry: [                                                                                                                                                          
      'logPageView',      // side effect — fires and forgets                                                                                                          
      'assignInitialData' // context update — pure, returns new context                                                                                               
    ]                                                                                                                                                                 
  });                                                                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                      
  const actions = {                                                                                                                                                   
    // Side effect: doesn't return anything, does something external                                                                                                  
    logPageView: () => analytics.track('page_view'),                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
    // Context update: pure, returns partial context                                                                                                                  
    assignInitialData: assign({ loaded: true, timestamp: Date.now() }),                                                                                               
  };                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
  If you want to visually distinguish these, the assign prefix does help. But you could also use:                                                                     
  - set* / update* for context                                                                                                                                        
  - do* / send* / plain verbs for effects                                                                                                                             
                                                                                                                                                                      
  My take                                                                                                                                                             
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Given your preference for verbs without boolean prefixes, I'd go with approach #2 (verb-based) and accept that you need to check the implementation to know if it's 
  an assign. The action name should describe what happens, not how.                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                                                      
  const actions = {                                                                                                                                                   
    setUser: assign({ ... }),                                                                                                                                         
    clearForm: assign({ ... }),                                                                                                                                       
    notifyParent: sendParent({ ... }),                                                                                                                                
    logError: (ctx, e) => console.error(e),                                                                                                                           
  };                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
  The type of action is an implementation detail. "Clear the form" is the intent — whether that's assign, a spawned actor, or something else is secondary.

⏺ 1. Imperative vs. observation events                                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                      
  The tension you've identified is real. Here's where it matters:                                                                                                     
                                                                                                                                                                      
  // User-initiated — imperative feels natural                                                                                                                        
  type UserEvents =                                                                                                                                                   
    | { type: 'SUBMIT' }                                                                                                                                              
    | { type: 'RETRY' }                                                                                                                                               
    | { type: 'CANCEL' }                                                                                                                                              
    | { type: 'CONNECT' }      // "please connect"                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                                      
  // System/external — past tense feels natural                                                                                                                       
  type SystemEvents =                                                                                                                                                 
    | { type: 'CONNECTED' }    // "wifi connected on its own"                                                                                                         
    | { type: 'DISCONNECTED' } // "connection dropped"                                                                                                                
    | { type: 'TIMEOUT' }      // "a timeout occurred"                                                                                                                
    | { type: 'TOKEN_EXPIRED' }                                                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                                                      
  A hybrid approach:                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
  // Commands (user/app intent)                                                                                                                                       
  'CONNECT' | 'DISCONNECT' | 'REFRESH' | 'AUTHENTICATE'                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                      
  // Notifications (external world informing the machine)                                                                                                             
  'CONNECTION_LOST' | 'SESSION_EXPIRED' | 'FILE_CHANGED' | 'QUOTA_EXCEEDED'                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                      
  The distinction: who initiates? Commands are sent to the machine to request action. Notifications are sent to the machine to inform it of external reality.         
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Some avoid this by using nouns for external events:                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                      
  'CONNECTION_LOSS' | 'SESSION_EXPIRY' | 'QUOTA_BREACH'                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                      
  But that can feel awkward. I think your instinct to use imperative for intent and past/noun for observation is sound — it encodes agency in the name.

  2. Nested state naming                                                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                      
  XState supports hierarchical states:                                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                      
  const authMachine = createMachine({                                                                                                                                 
    initial: 'unauthenticated',                                                                                                                                       
    states: {                                                                                                                                                         
      unauthenticated: {                                                                                                                                              
        initial: 'idle',                                                                                                                                              
        states: {                                                                                                                                                     
          idle: {},                                                                                                                                                   
          authenticating: {},                                                                                                                                         
          error: {},                                                                                                                                                  
        }                                                                                                                                                             
      },                                                                                                                                                              
      authenticated: {                                                                                                                                                
        initial: 'idle',                                                                                                                                              
        states: {                                                                                                                                                     
          idle: {},                                                                                                                                                   
          loadingProfile: {},   // explicit context                                                                                                                   
          // vs.                                                                                                                                                      
          loading: {},          // relies on parent for context                                                                                                       
        }                                                                                                                                                             
      }                                                                                                                                                               
    }                                                                                                                                                                 
  });                                                                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                      
  With loading, you check:                                                                                                                                            
  state.matches('authenticated.loading')  // parent provides context                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
  With loadingProfile:                                                                                                                                                
  state.matches('authenticated.loadingProfile')  // self-documenting but redundant                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Flat naming trusts the hierarchy:                                                                                                                                   
  authenticated.idle                                                                                                                                                  
  authenticated.loading                                                                                                                                               
  authenticated.error                                                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Explicit naming is defensive:                                                                                                                                       
  authenticated.idle                                                                                                                                                  
  authenticated.loadingProfile                                                                                                                                        
  authenticated.profileError                                                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                      
  The tradeoff: explicit survives refactoring (if you move the state elsewhere, the name still makes sense) but violates DRY. Flat naming is cleaner but requires you 
  to read the hierarchy.

  4. Namespacing and guards                                                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Guards are predicates that control whether a transition can fire:                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                                                      
  const machine = createMachine({                                                                                                                                     
    on: {                                                                                                                                                             
      SUBMIT: {                                                                                                                                                       
        target: 'submitting',                                                                                                                                         
        guard: 'formComplete',  // only transition if this returns true                                                                                               
      }                                                                                                                                                               
    }                                                                                                                                                                 
  });                                                                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Flat naming:                                                                                                                                                        
  const guards = {                                                                                                                                                    
    formComplete: (ctx) => ctx.name && ctx.email,                                                                                                                     
    formPristine: (ctx) => !ctx.touched,                                                                                                                              
    userAuthorized: (ctx) => ctx.user?.role === 'admin',                                                                                                              
    retriesRemaining: (ctx) => ctx.retries < 3,                                                                                                                       
  };                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
  const actions = {                                                                                                                                                   
    clearForm: assign({ name: '', email: '' }),                                                                                                                       
    validateForm: assign({ errors: (ctx) => validate(ctx) }),                                                                                                         
    logFormSubmit: () => analytics.track('form_submit'),                                                                                                              
    notifyUser: (ctx) => toast(ctx.message),                                                                                                                          
  };                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Grouped namespacing:                                                                                                                                                
  const guards = {                                                                                                                                                    
    form: {                                                                                                                                                           
      complete: (ctx) => ctx.name && ctx.email,                                                                                                                       
      pristine: (ctx) => !ctx.touched,                                                                                                                                
    },                                                                                                                                                                
    user: {                                                                                                                                                           
      authorized: (ctx) => ctx.user?.role === 'admin',                                                                                                                
    },                                                                                                                                                                
    retries: {                                                                                                                                                        
      remaining: (ctx) => ctx.retries < 3,                                                                                                                            
    },                                                                                                                                                                
  };                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
  const actions = {                                                                                                                                                   
    form: {                                                                                                                                                           
      clear: assign({ name: '', email: '' }),                                                                                                                         
      validate: assign({ errors: (ctx) => validate(ctx) }),                                                                                                           
      logSubmit: () => analytics.track('form_submit'),                                                                                                                
    },                                                                                                                                                                
    user: {                                                                                                                                                           
      notify: (ctx) => toast(ctx.message),                                                                                                                            
    },                                                                                                                                                                
  };                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Usage in machine differs:                                                                                                                                           
  // Flat                                                                                                                                                             
  guard: 'formComplete'                                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                      
  // Grouped (XState v5 style)                                                                                                                                        
  guard: 'form.complete'                                                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Grouped scales better for large machines and lets you see at a glance what domain an action/guard belongs to.

  5. Negation at call site                                                                                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                                      
  XState v5 supports this directly:                                                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                                                      
  // Define only the positive                                                                                                                                         
  const guards = {                                                                                                                                                    
    authorized: (ctx) => ctx.user?.permissions.includes('write'),                                                                                                     
  };                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                      
  // Negate at call site                                                                                                                                              
  on: {                                                                                                                                                               
    DELETE: [                                                                                                                                                         
      { target: 'error', guard: { type: 'not', guard: 'authorized' } },                                                                                               
      { target: 'deleting', guard: 'authorized' },                                                                                                                    
    ]                                                                                                                                                                 
  }                                                                                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Or with a helper:                                                                                                                                                   
  const not = (guard: string) => ({ type: 'not', guard });                                                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                                      
  on: {                                                                                                                                                               
    DELETE: [                                                                                                                                                         
      { target: 'error', guard: not('authorized') },                                                                                                                  
      { target: 'deleting', guard: 'authorized' },                                                                                                                    
    ]                                                                                                                                                                 
  }                                                                                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                                                      
  This keeps your guard definitions clean — one source of truth for the concept.

  6. Adjective vs. noun for terminal states                                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                      
  Adjectives (loaded, complete, authenticated):                                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                                                      
  states: {                                                                                                                                                           
    idle: {},                                                                                                                                                         
    loading: {},

    loading: {},

    failure: {},

  cohesive.                                                                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                      
  ---                                                                                                                                                                 
  Hybrid (what I often see):                                                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                      
  states: {                                                                                                                                                           
    idle: {},                                                                                                                                                         
    loading: {},                                                                                                                                                      
    ready: {},     // adjective — data loaded, ready to use                                                                                                           
    error: {},     // noun — but universally understood                                                                                                               
  }                                                                                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                                                      
  error as a noun is so idiomatic that it transcends the grammar debate. ready sidesteps the loaded/fetched awkwardness.

  The distinction                                                                                                                                                     
  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────┬──────────────────┬──────────────────┐                                                                                   
  │               State type                │    Character     │      Naming      │                                                                                   
  ├─────────────────────────────────────────┼──────────────────┼──────────────────┤                                                                                   
  │ Inactive, dormant, awaiting first input │ Static condition │ idle (adjective) │                                                                                   
  ├─────────────────────────────────────────┼──────────────────┼──────────────────┤                                                                                   
  │ Active but unoccupied, between tasks    │ Ongoing activity │ idling (gerund)  │                                                                                   
  └─────────────────────────────────────────┴──────────────────┴──────────────────┘                                                                                   
  Or, alternative framings for the "between tasks" state:                                                                                                             
                                                                                                                                                                      
  - waiting — generic, implies expectation                                                                                                                            
  - listening — for event-driven machines (sockets, streams)                                                                                                          
  - ready — implies capability, "ready to act"                                                                                                                        
  - standing (as in "standing by") — military/aviation flavor                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                                                      
  ---                                                                                                                                                                 
  My take                                                                                                                                                             
                                                                                                                                                                      
  idling is more consistent with your system, but idle is deeply conventional. You could:                                                                             
                                                                                                                                                                      
  1. Use idling everywhere — consistency wins                                                                                                                         
  2. Use idle for initial states, idling for "between work" states — semantic distinction                                                                             
  3. Avoid both — use waiting, ready, or domain-specific terms (listening, standby)
