# Interview: Pi First Look recap
Date: 2026-03-25
Source: recap of "Pi First Look" YouTube video
Output: `recap-v2.md`, 5 seed posts

## Round 1

### Q: What was the REAL reason you kept not trying Pi for a month?
**A:** Knew it would consume me. "Literally everything. Vim, Automatic Watches, Power Tools, Keyboard layouts, microphones, PC building. Anything and everything."

### Q: What's your actual Vim origin story?
**A:** Started around 2009 when switching from QWERTY to Dvorak. Moving into a software dev career. Tired of favorite editors becoming unsupported (TextMate, SublimeText 2). Investment in Vim = transferrable productivity. Likes free software (Stallman definition — free as in liberated). Hates being locked into an ecosystem.

### Q: How real is the Anthropic fear?
**A:** "Feels founded to me. They block OpenCode and OpenClaw. Claude Code is their sticky product and they don't want others to get that sticky feeling with another agent/harness."

### Q: What does Pi MEAN to you beyond features?
**A:** "Building against a baseline harness forces me to build against the least common denominator. So my skills (both personal and agentic) are portable/transferrable. And in the event that I really need to unload Agent Teams (with Claude Code), I can 'upgrade' into that path selectively. Additionally, doing things on hard mode requires me to really understand the technology in a way that simply utilizing features doesn't."

### Q: Is there a life parallel to "built too much on one thing"?
**A:** "Not sure how related but I do feel the fear of replacability - irrelevance. When I feel that way, I've always looked for comfort a layer below my typical operation. It means that I'm more capable than average. Understanding is always the unlock."

## Round 2

### Q: Has the consumption pattern burned you before? Specific example?
**A:** "Literally everything. Vim, Automatic Watches, Power Tools, Keyboard layouts, microphones, PC building. Anything and everything."

### Q: "A layer below" — concrete example of going deeper when feeling irrelevant?
**A:** "Learning React was this way for me. Everyone was building libraries and augmentations, and I pressed into understanding React fundamentals. And as all those libraries started to lose favor or come up short (long-term), hunkering down on React panned out. This is the same with Vim. I like to say 'I can be productive in *all* editors now (at least those with good Vim emulation)'. Financially, I'm a very fundamentals type person. I go by the numbers. Dave Ramsey Baby Steps or GTFO. For me, freedom is being capable. And yeah, I get the irony that none of my skills are hard/survival skills. But we're talking about feelings, not facts."

### Q: Dvorak AND Vim at the same time — deliberate?
**A:** "Deliberate. I'm a glutton for punishment and my mind was made up. But also, Vim is more muscle memory than pneumonic. I knew I didn't want to stay on QWERTY. And if I was learning a new thing, didn't make sense to wait. Also, I made this switch while my newborn was in the hospital with pediatric cancer. Grasping for control I guess. That's probably another good example."

### Q: The 20-minute time limit — real or performance?
**A:** "Mostly for the video. I think I recorded for about an hour. But I've been using Pi to build skills and production pipelines nonstop since."
